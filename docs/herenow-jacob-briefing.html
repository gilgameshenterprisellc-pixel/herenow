Overview

What HereNow Is

HereNow is an IRL social layer for physical venues. It's not a review app, not a check-in gimmick, and not another social feed. It's a presence platform — when you walk into a participating venue, the app knows you're there and unlocks a real-time social experience tied to that space.

Users check in, see who else is at the same venue, send connection requests to people they actually met in person, message them through a time-limited DM window, and earn badges the more places they explore. Venue owners get a real-time dashboard, can run promotions, post announcements, schedule events, and see who's in the building.

The core idea: be physically present, be rewarded for it, and meet people in the real world through the app — not the other way around.

New User

What a New User Experiences

1

Download & Sign Up

User opens the app, creates an account with email and password, sets a display name and profile photo. They're dropped onto the home screen immediately — no lengthy onboarding.

2

Home Screen — Nearby Venues

The home tab shows a live list of venues within 50km sorted by distance. Each venue card shows how many people are there right now, venue chips (like "Cocktails" or "Live Music"), distance, and a "LIVE" badge if anyone is checked in. There's a search bar and a Trending strip showing the busiest venues at that moment.

3

Arriving at a Venue — Geofenced Check-In

When the user physically walks to Martha My Dear, the app's background location detects they're within range and shows a check-in prompt. They tap to check in. The app runs a precise polygon check — if they're inside the building footprint, the check-in succeeds. If they're standing outside in the parking lot, it won't. No QR codes, no codes to enter, just show up.

4

Inside the Venue — Four Tabs Unlock

Pulse — A live social feed just for this venue. Post a vibe, a photo moment, or a quick thought. Posts are venue-scoped and disappear when you leave. People — See everyone else checked in right now. Each person shows their display name, social mode (Open/Selective/Not Today), and their top badge. Chat — A live group chat room for the venue. Everyone checked in can see it. Events — Upcoming events the venue has scheduled, open to anyone whether checked in or not.

5

Meeting Someone — We Met

If a user sees someone in the People tab they actually talked to, they send a "We Met" request. If the other person confirms it, both sides unlock a direct message thread. By default the thread expires in 72 hours — creating urgency to keep the conversation going. If both people reply at least once, the connection becomes permanent and never expires.

6

Messages Tab

All DM threads live in the Messages tab (mail icon in the tab bar). Threads show the other person's name, last message preview, how long until expiry (🔒 = still at venue, "4h left" = expiring soon, red = expired), and an unread badge. Tapping opens the conversation. The mail icon in the tab bar shows total unread count in real time.

7

Updates Tab

The bell icon tab shows two things: promos and announcements from venues the user subscribes to, and system activity (We Met confirmations, badges earned). The bell icon shows a badge count for unread activity.

8

Badges & Profile

Every action earns toward badges: first check-in, Explorer (5 venues), Explorer II (15), Explorer III (50), Adventurer (3 different venues), Martha My Dear Regular (5+ visits at the same spot), Social Butterfly, Connector, Pulse Master, Photographer, Night Owl, and more. All beta users automatically receive the Founding Member badge. The profile screen shows their badge count, streak, and earned badges grouped by category with a progress bar.

9

Ghost Mode

Users can toggle Ghost Mode (mood = "Not Today") from their profile. When active, a red banner appears across the top of the app so they know it's on. In Ghost Mode, their name won't appear in the venue's People tab — they're present but invisible to others.

Venue Owner

What a Venue Owner Experiences

1

Apply for a Venue

A venue owner creates a regular user account, then navigates to Create Venue. They submit the venue name, address, type (bar, restaurant, etc.), and description. The submission goes into a Pending queue visible in the Admin panel. Joshua reviews and approves or rejects.

2

Approval & Geofencing

Once approved, the venue goes live on the map. The Admin panel automatically attempts to pull the building's polygon footprint from OpenStreetMap. If found, check-ins are restricted to the precise building shape. If not found, a circle radius is used as fallback. For Martha My Dear, the polygon has been manually drawn and is active — check-in is polygon-gated to the exact building.

3

Live Dashboard

The venue owner's dashboard shows: Live Now (real-time check-in count with pulse animation), Today's Activity (today vs. yesterday with % change), Customer Mix (new vs. returning visitors with a proportion bar), a 7-day bar chart, Peak Hours (top 3 busiest times), event count, announcement count, subscriber count, and age/interest breakdowns. All aggregated — no PII exposed.

4

Promotion Performance

Each active promotion tracks how many users have viewed it. The dashboard shows view counts per promotion sorted by most-viewed, so venue owners can see which promos are getting traction. Promotions can have optional start and end dates for time-window scheduling.

5

Event Performance

Each event shows RSVP count and how many actual check-ins occurred during the event window — the difference between people who were interested and people who actually showed up.

6

Live People Tab

A real-time guest list showing every checked-in user: their name, social mode, and arrival time. Next to each guest, the owner can see which promotions they've claimed and mark them redeemed (or un-redeem if needed). Green chip = redeemed. This replaces the need for staff to manually track who got what deal.

7

Announcements, Events & Promotions

From the dashboard the owner can post venue announcements (text + optional image) that appear in the Pulse feed and push to subscribers' Updates tab. They can schedule events with a title, description, and start time. Promotions can be created with custom text and a time window — users see them when browsing the venue or in the Updates tab.

8

Venue Gallery

The venue has its own photo gallery. The owner can upload photos directly. Regular users can submit photos for owner review — the owner approves or rejects each submission before it goes live. Photos display in a 2-column Instagram-style grid on the venue detail page.

9

Temporarily Closed

If the venue is closed for a private event, renovation, holiday, etc., the owner can toggle "Temporarily Closed" from their admin panel. A custom message can be set (e.g. "Closed for private event — back Friday at 5pm"). On the map, the venue card goes grey with a CLOSED pill and shows the message. Check-ins are not allowed while closed.

Testing

How to Test Right Now (iOS)

Jacob is on iPhone.

The app isn't on the App Store yet — that requires Apple's TestFlight process. For now, Jacob tests via Expo Go, which lets him run the full app directly from Joshua's development server over the internet.

1

Jacob installs Expo Go on iPhone

App Store → search "Expo Go" → install. Free, no account required. This is a shell app that can run any Expo project when given a link.

2

Joshua starts the tunnel server

Joshua opens VS Code on his desktop, navigates to the HereNow project folder, and runs:

npx expo start --tunnel

After 15–20 seconds a URL appears in the terminal that looks like:
exp://cpn4cg4-gilgameshenterprisellc-8081.exp.direct
Joshua copies this and texts it to Jacob.

3

Jacob opens the link on his iPhone

Jacob taps the link in iMessage. It opens Expo Go and loads the full HereNow app. He's now running the latest code on his phone. Joshua must keep VS Code running for the entire test session — if he closes the terminal or laptop goes to sleep, Jacob's connection drops.

4

What works vs. what doesn't in Expo Go

Everything works: check-in, Pulse, People, Chat, Messages, We Met, badges, venue dashboard, gallery, settings. The one thing that doesn't render on iPhone is the visual map (the venue list and all other features still work without it). Background location runs slightly differently in Expo Go vs. a production build but is functional for testing.

Note on the tunnel URL:

The URL changes every time Joshua starts the tunnel. He needs to text Jacob a new link each session. This is a limitation of the development server — it's not how the final app will work.

What's Next

Incoming Steps

Priority 1

TestFlight (iOS proper distribution)

Right now Jacob tests via Expo Go + tunnel URL, which requires Joshua to have his laptop running the whole time. TestFlight is Apple's official beta distribution platform — Jacob installs the app once from a link and it works standalone forever, no laptop needed. Requires an Apple Developer Program membership ($99/yr). Once enrolled, Joshua builds a production IPA, uploads to App Store Connect, adds Jacob as a tester, and Jacob gets a single install link.

Priority 2

Native Map on iPhone

The interactive venue map is currently web-only. On iPhone, users see the venue list but no visual map. Building the native map uses react-native-maps (a well-supported library) with MapKit on iOS. This unlocks the animated venue glow, pin tapping, and the polygon boundary overlay on Jacob's phone. Medium-sized build.

Priority 3

More Venues

Martha My Dear is the only live venue right now. The app gets significantly more interesting with 3–5 venues in the same city. Any Nashville bar, restaurant, or venue can apply through the app. Joshua approves from the Admin panel. Each new venue immediately gets geofencing, check-in, dashboard, and all features.

Priority 4

Real Users at Martha

The best test is real people using it in the actual venue on a real night. Even a handful of users checking in together surfaces issues that development testing can't catch — concurrent check-ins, real-time Pulse, the We Met flow between strangers, DM expiry behavior. Worth coordinating a soft launch night at Martha with a small invited crowd.

Priority 5

Push Notifications

The in-app notification system is complete. Push notifications (the kind that pop up on your lock screen) need VAPID keys set in the server environment. Once configured, users get notified of We Met requests, badge unlocks, venue announcements, and DM messages even when the app is closed. Essential for real engagement.

Priority 6

App Store (iOS public)

Full App Store submission requires the same Apple Developer account as TestFlight plus a completed App Store listing (screenshots, description, privacy policy, age rating). Can be done once TestFlight is stable and the app has been tested with real users. 3–7 day Apple review period.
