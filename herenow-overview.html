What HereNow Is

The idea

IRL presence layer

Users check into a venue — a bar, coffee shop, event space — and see who else is there right now. They set a Social Mode and a Mood, and if something real happens, they tap "We Met." That unlocks a 72-hour message window after they both leave.

What sets it apart

No algorithms. No followers.

HereNow is entirely in-the-moment. There are no public feeds to game, no follower counts to chase. Everything expires. The app exists to make things happen IRL — not to keep you scrolling.

Main app — 4 tabs

Nearby · Feed · Notifications · Profile

The core experience once signed in. Nearby shows venues around you. Feed is global. Notifications handles We Met requests, messages, and events.

Inside a venue

People · Pulse · Chat · Events

Four tabs inside every zone. See who's checked in, drop ephemeral Pulse posts (12h expiry), join live venue chat, and browse upcoming events.

Connection flow

We Met → 72h DM window

Both people tap We Met at the venue. Messages stay locked while they're present. When they leave, the DM window opens for 72 hours — then it closes.

Afterglow

When a user checks out, they see an auto-generated recap: how long they were there, how many people they were around, and how many connections they made.

Venue owners

Venue owners get their own home: a live check-in counter, demographic breakdowns, top interest tags, and quick actions to manage their space and events.

Person Signup — Step by Step

This is the exact experience a new user has from first open to being inside a venue.

1
Landing page

Opening the app on web shows the HereNow landing page — animated background, neon wordmark, and a brief explanation of what the app is. Two CTAs: "Get Started" and "I own a venue."

2
Sign up

The signup screen shows a Person / Venue toggle at the top. Person is the default. The user enters a display name, username, email, and password, then taps "Create Account."

3
Profile setup

After account creation, the user lands on their profile editor. They add a bio, choose an age range, pick up to 16 interest tags from a grid, and write a kickoff prompt — a one-liner about why they're out tonight. Avatar upload is available on web.

→ Tapping "Save & Continue" moves them into the main app.
4
Onboarding tour (first launch only)

A 5-slide modal walks through the core concepts: Social Mode, Mood Mode, how We Met works, and what Afterglow is. It only appears once and never shows again on future opens.

5
Nearby tab

The user lands on the Nearby screen and sees venues around their current location. Tapping a venue card opens the zone detail where they can read about it and check in.

6
Check in

The user taps "Check In" from the zone detail. They choose their Social Mode (Dating / Friends / Networking / Just Vibes) and their Mood (Open / Selective / Not Today). These are visible to other people checked in — they signal intent without a word.

→ They are now inside the venue.
7
Inside the venue

Four tabs become available: People (everyone checked in right now), Pulse (ephemeral posts, 12h), Chat (live group chat for that venue), and Events. Users can browse who else is there and see their modes and interest tags.

8
We Met

If the user wants to connect with someone, they tap "We Met" on that person's card. The other person gets a notification and taps to confirm. While both are still at the venue, messages stay locked — that's intentional. It keeps the focus on the real-life moment, not the phone.

9
Checkout → Afterglow → DMs open

When the user checks out, the Afterglow screen shows: time spent, how many people they crossed paths with, and how many We Met connections they made. Any confirmed We Mets unlock a 72-hour DM window — which then closes.

→ Messages are now available in the Messages tab.

Venue Signup — What a Venue Experiences

Venue sign-ups go through a review process before their space goes live on the map.

1
Venue toggle on signup

On the signup screen, the venue owner taps the "Venue" toggle. The form switches to venue-specific fields: venue name, venue type (Bar / Coffee Shop / Restaurant / Event Space / Music Venue / Gym / Co-working / Other), email, and password.

2
Account created — pending review

After tapping "Create Account," the venue owner is routed to their dashboard. Their account is created immediately, but their space is not yet visible on the map to users.

⏱ Their dashboard shows a pending state: "Thank you for your interest in HereNow — your venue is under review. We'll be in touch shortly."
3
Review & approval

A HereNow admin reviews the submission and approves the venue. The venue owner is notified. Their space is now cleared to go live.

4
Venue goes live

The owner opens their Edit Venue screen, taps "Use My Current Location" while standing at their space, selects a radius that fits the venue size, and saves. Their venue is now visible to nearby users and ready to receive check-ins.

→ The live dashboard now shows real-time check-in counts and stats.

Under the Hood

Platform

Web & Native Mobile

HereNow runs on web today for rapid preview and testing. The same codebase compiles to a native iOS and Android app when ready for app store submission — no rebuild required.

Real-time

Live updates everywhere

Venue chat, Pulse posts, check-in counts, and We Met notifications all update in real time. Nothing requires a manual refresh.

Location

GPS geofencing

Background geofencing means when a user enters a venue's radius, they get a prompt to check in automatically. Venue owners set their own location in one tap from inside the app — no coordinates needed.

Privacy by design

Everything expires

Pulse posts expire in 12 hours. Venue chat clears in 24 hours. We Met DMs close after 72 hours. The app is designed to be ephemeral — not to archive your social life.
